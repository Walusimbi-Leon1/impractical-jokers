About This Book

Impractical Jokers premiered on December 15, 2011, and quickly became the most popular program on TruTV. What started as a simple hidden-camera show evolved into a global comedy phenomenon — a show about four lifelong friends from Staten Island who compete to embarrass each other in front of unsuspecting strangers.

"We wanted to utilize our improv strengths... put ourselves into a situation where we know we can be funny and also ourselves. If we're ourselves and also showcase our friendship, something might resonate there." — Sal Vulcano

This book tells the complete story of Impractical Jokers — from the origins of The Tenderloins comedy troupe at Monsignor Farrell High School, through twelve seasons of television and a feature film, to the show's evolution after Joe Gatto's departure. We explore each Joker individually, dissect the show's format and philosophy, celebrate the fan community, and examine the show's lasting impact on comedy television.

Whether you're a lifelong fan or discovering the show for the first time, this guide offers a deep dive into everything that makes Impractical Jokers one of the most genuinely funny and heartwarming shows ever made.
